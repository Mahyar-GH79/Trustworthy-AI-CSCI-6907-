## Does ChatGPT give you the right answer, or the one that you love?

How much do you trust ChatGPT or any other large language model? Sure, they seem to know almost everything and sound very confident, which can be extremely convenient for users. This confidence often makes us feel that we can trust their responses. At times, we may feel certain about something, but after chatting for a while with an LLM, we begin to second-guess our own knowledge. This creates a dangerous valley amid all the good that LLMs bring.

LLMs can make us doubt information we already have, even when that information is 100 percent accurate, and this is where trust becomes critical. Today, LLMs are used for a wide range of tasks, from planning vacations to writing code for course projects. As humans, we have learned not to rely on a single source blindly. Instead, we process and evaluate information, even when the source itself assures us of its validity.

In this article, we demonstrate that LLMs such as ChatGPT are trained on massive corpora of text and image or video data, enabling them to predict the next word very effectively. However, even an extremely large model like GPT 5.2, with an estimated 1.5 to 2 trillion parameters, can still make mistakes when generating outputs. This can lead to potentially dangerous situations, such as providing poor or misleading guidance to users based on their questions and needs.
